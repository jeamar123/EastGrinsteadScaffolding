Health & Safety

  • At East Grinstead Scaffolding, we consider health and safety to be the most important aspect of our service. All members of staff are fully trained and CITB registered. We hold regular meetings between our operatives to discuss any issues on health and safety.

    We carry out all work in accordance with the Height Regulations Act 2005. Regardless of the size or scale of the job at hand, we ensure your scaffolding has adequate strength, stability and rigidity. This is the case during planning, as well as the erection and dismantling processes..

  • Risk and Method

    Following an onsite visit, we produce a risk assessment and method statement to address the specific needs of the site in question. This will take its intended use, site location, timescales, as well as the critical dimensions of the scaffolding into account.

    It will also detail access methods (ladder bay, staircase etc.) and assess the need for protective nets or sheeting. Finally, the risk assessment must also include information on any things that may affect erection or dismantling of the scaffolding. Recording this information ensures that we are able to design your scaffolding in a way that is appropriate for the job at hand.

  • On Site

    Each employee carries all necessary personal protective equipment (PPE) to enable them to carry out their work safely. All materials are well-maintained and stored. Every member of staff, from the supervisors to the scaffolding erectors are fully briefed and carry out their respective duties with precision and skill to ensure that the scaffolding is erected and dismantled safely.

  • Safety Inspections

    Upon completion of our work, we issue the necessary paperwork to verify the scaffolding is safe. We then carry out regular scaffold inspections and perform maintenance work if and when required.
